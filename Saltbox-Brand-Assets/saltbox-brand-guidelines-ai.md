## 1. BRAND DNA

### 1.1 Mission
**Statement:** "Democratize access to logistics for all"

**Core belief:** Small businesses are too important to lose. Must find innovative ways to democratize access to critical logistical capabilities that our modern economy demands.

**Three key insights:**
1. Customer expectations are rising, small businesses pushed to fringes of logistics industry
2. Customers "suffering" from their own success - growth brings complexity and logistics headaches
3. Entrepreneurship is lonely - relationships and trust are paramount

### 1.2 Positioning
**Tagline:** "Your logistics partner from first sale to full scale"

**What we do:**
- Bring enterprise-level services to SMBs at scale
- Enable small merchants to effectively compete
- Thrive in high-expectation world

**Value propositions:**
1. Human-centric logistics, making entrepreneurship less lonely
2. Breaking norms with spaces built with design and purpose in mind (colorful walls, murals, inspirational quotes, vibrant loading docks)
3. Make logistics simple and modern

**Differentiation:** Support for entrepreneurs goes above and beyond. Offer connection, mentorship, friendship in modern digital era.

### 1.3 Core Values
**Four values (apply to all communications):**

1. **Member-centric**
   - Everything for members and merchants
   - When facing obstacles, think about what's best for members first

2. **Innovative**
   - Breaking norms and trailblazing into new realms
   - Enable better access to logistics for all businesses
   - World evolving fast, so are we

3. **Community-focused**
   - Heart of small business lives in communities
   - Ensure communities don't lose heartbeat in modern digital era

4. **Approachable**
   - Starting/growing/scaling a small business is no small feat
   - Strive to be industry experts to support members every step
   - No question is dumb or too complex

### 1.4 Voice Attributes
**Four voice characteristics (use in all messaging):**

1. **Rational**
   - Keep it real
   - Use straight talk, focus on solving customers' actual problems
   - Highly analytical
   - Capture valuable data and insights from and for customers

2. **Ambitious**
   - Never settle for "good enough"
   - Always striving to build better solutions to fulfill needs of modern merchants
   - Also ambitious on behalf of customers
   - Help them break through barriers

3. **Likeable**
   - Customers think of us like a trusted friend
   - Tone: consistently inviting and approachable
   - From design of locations to joyful dispositions of staff
   - Bring lightness, joy, and humor to a journey otherwise full of challenges

4. **Empathetic**
   - Trust is the foundation
   - Build trust by showing we understand unique needs and challenges of modern merchants
   - Evolve solutions in response to those needs
   - Understand their journey and deliver what they need

### 1.5 Tone
**How we speak (personality):**

1. **Inviting**
   - We are logistics experts
   - Use knowledge to support business owners at every stage

2. **Energizing**
   - Infuse words with passion (but won't go wild with puns or exclamation marks)

3. **Conversational**
   - Acknowledge weight of starting a business in a way that feels comfortable and casual

4. **Sincere**
   - Speak about offerings with authenticity
   - Always bring passion and energy into conversations and copy

### 1.6 Target Audience
**Primary segments:**

1. **Ecommerce Merchants**
   - Target segment: merchants on platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, Squarespace, Amazon
   - Most likely higher, consistent shipping volume
   - Contribute to North Star (ship as many packages as possible)

2. **Makers & Crafters**
   - Entrepreneurs who need space to make, create, and craft their products
   - They may ship, they may not

3. **Agencies**
   - Agencies and groups who enable merchants and businesses to scale with their services

4. **Professionals**
   - Independents, freelancers, ecommerce professionals who enable merchants/businesses to scale

## 2. CORPORATE IDENTITY

### 2.1 Logo Composition
**Components:**
- **Wordmark:** "saltbox" (always lowercase)
- **Brandmark:** Semi-circle "sun" symbol integrated into the "o"

**Logo = Wordmark + Brandmark combined**

**Design principles:**
- Refined every detail for clarity
- Designed for timeless appeal
- Colors enhance recognition
- Scalability across all media

### 2.2 Clearance Requirements
**Rule:** Minimum buffer space all around = height of letter "b"

**Purpose:** Exclusion zone ensures no other graphic elements interfere with legibility or impact

### 2.3 Minimum Sizes
**Print media:**
- Full logo (wordmark): 25mm minimum
- Brandmark alone: 10mm minimum

**Digital media:**
- Full logo (wordmark): 100px minimum
- Brandmark alone: 34px minimum

**Purpose:** Maintain integrity and deliver intended impact

### 2.4 Color Variations

**Text variations available:**
- Light backgrounds: Black 100 text + Orange brandmark
- Orange backgrounds: Black 10 (dark navy) or White text + White or Black brandmark
- Dark backgrounds (Black 100): White text + Orange or Yellow brandmark

**CONSTRAINT: Use only approved color combinations. Do not improvise with other colors.**

### 2.5 Logo Misuses
**NEVER do the following:**

1. ❌ Add strokes or outlines to logo shape
2. ❌ Stick to core brand colors. No improvising with other colors.
3. ❌ Keep brand colors bold and true. Lighter shades are off-limits.
4. ❌ Maintain logo's friendly voice. Never use uppercase for the name.
5. ❌ The sun symbol is sacred. Don't resize or distort its proportions.
6. ❌ Preserve the logo's integrity. No stretching, squishing, or alterations.

### 2.6 Partner Lockups
**When collaborating with partner brands:**

**Arrangement options:**
- **Horizontal:** Join logos with + symbol, symbolizing partnership and collaboration
- **Stacked:** Logos arranged vertically with + symbol between

**Rules:**
- Never alter colors or opacity of partner logo
- Both brands should shine equally in their original form
- Achieve clear and balanced look

**Alignment guidance:**
- Use Inter Thin, Black 90
- Half the cap height of Saltbox logo
- Vertically/horizontally center-aligned

## 3. TYPOGRAPHY

### 3.1 Primary Font: Inter Tight
**Usage:** Headlines

**Weights available:**
- Thin
- Extra Light
- Light
- Regular
- Medium
- Semi Bold
- Bold

**CRITICAL RULE: Always add 2px of letter spacing for Inter Tight**

**Characteristics:** Balanced spacing that looks bold and sharp. More open spacing for easy reading at larger sizes.

### 3.2 Primary Font: Inter
**Usage:** Body text

**Weights available:**
- Thin
- Extra Light
- Light
- Regular
- Medium
- Semi Bold
- Bold

**Characteristics:** Simple, clean, functional. More open spacing for easy reading.

### 3.3 Secondary Font: Besley
**Usage:** Headlines & Callouts

**Weights available:**
- Regular Italic
- Medium Italic

**Characteristics:** Touch of sophistication. Adds warmth and human touch. Perfect for moments calling for extra charm. Always use in italic form to maintain refined and intentional style.

### 3.4 Fallback Fonts
**When primary fonts unavailable:**

**Headlines Serif:** Baskerville
- Regular Italic
- Semi-Bold Italic

**Headlines Sans Serif:** Arial
- Regular
- Bold

**Body text:** Arial
- Regular
- Bold

**Purpose:** Maintain brand's clean, professional look while ensuring readability across all platforms

### 3.5 Heading Sizes & Hierarchy

**CRITICAL RULE: All headlines are set in sentence case**

| Level | Font Size | Line Height | Usage Context |
|-------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| H1 | 56px / 3.5rem | 120% | Main page titles |
| H2 | 48px / 3rem | 120% | Major section headers |
| H3 | 40px / 2.5rem | 120% | Subsection headers |
| H4 | 32px / 2rem | 130% | Minor headers |
| H5 | 24px / 1.5rem | 140% | Small headers |
| H6 | 20px / 1.25rem | 140% | Smallest headers |

**Font pairing for headlines:**
- Most cases: Inter Tight and Besley italic
- Not a hard rule for blog posts or longer headlines
- Use Besley intentionally to emphasize a singular word, verb, or adjective

### 3.6 Body Text Sizes

| Size Name | Font Size | Line Height | Weight Options |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Text X large | 24px / 1.25rem | 150% | Bold, Semi Bold, Medium, Regular, Light |
| Text large | 20px / 1.25rem | 150% | Bold, Semi Bold, Medium, Regular, Light |
| Text medium | 18px / 1.125rem | 150% | Bold, Semi Bold, Medium, Regular, Light |
| Text regular | 16px / 1rem | 150% | Bold, Semi Bold, Medium, Regular, Light |
| Text small | 14px / 0.875rem | 150% | Bold, Semi Bold, Medium, Regular, Light |
| Text tiny | 12px / 0.75rem | 150% | Bold, Semi Bold, Medium, Regular |

### 3.7 Typography Hierarchy Pattern

**Standard content structure:**

1. **TAG** - Body Large, Inter Regular, UPPER CASE
2. **HEADLINE** - H2, Inter Tight Regular and Besley Regular Italic, Sentence case
3. **PARAGRAPH** - Body Medium, Inter Regular
4. **PARAGRAPH BOLD** - Body Medium, Inter Semi-Bold
5. **CALLOUT** - Body XLarge, Inter Semi-Bold
6. **SIGNOFF** - H6, Besley Medium Italic

### 3.8 Leading & Spacing Rules

**Spacing:**
- Always use consistent spacing before and after paragraphs to avoid cluttered or uneven layout
- Apply same rules across all platforms for unified, polished appearance

**Leading (Line Height):**
- Ensure line height complements font size
- Provide enough space for easy reading without feeling too loose
- For accessibility: minimum 1.5x (150%) leading for body text
- Headlines and large callouts: 120%-140%

**Alignment:**
- Stick to left-aligned text

### 3.9 Typography Best Practices

**Do:**
- Avoid widows and orphans (adjust spacing or line breaks to maintain balanced layout)
- Maintain line length 45-75 characters for optimal readability
- Prioritize leading (use enough space between lines)
- Balance white space (leave enough margin and padding around text)
- Use consistent alignment (left-aligned for modern, approachable look)
- Choose proper caps usage (reserve all caps for tags or specific callouts)

**Don't:**
- No hyphenation (avoid breaking words with hyphens; adjust text size or tracking)
- Avoid overuse of bold or italics (use sparingly for emphasis without overwhelming design)
- Don't overcrowd fonts (stick to approved font styles; adding additional font families can clash)

**Accessibility:**
- Set type in colors meeting WCAG standards for contrast
- Ensure text easily readable against background
- Contrast ratio: at least 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text

### 5.3 Photo Categories

#### LIFESTYLE PHOTOGRAPHY
**Purpose:** Captures the heart of our community—entrepreneurs who make, create, and craft with passion

**Requirements:**
- Feel authentic and natural
- Never overly posed or staged
- Showcase customers in their element:
  - Deep in focus at their workbench
  - Packaging products for shipment
  - Sharing a lighthearted moment with others in their workspace

**Goal:** Tell real stories of creativity and connection, highlighting the joy and determination that drive their work

**Tone:** Every shot should feel **genuine**, celebrating the vibrant energy of people doing what they love

#### SPACES PHOTOGRAPHY
**Purpose:** Show off the amenities that make us stand out

**What to capture:**
- Sleek conference rooms
- Orange areas
- Fully stocked kitchens
- Content studios
- Loading docks
- Warehouse suites of all sizes

**Style requirements:**
- Sharp, bright, and uncluttered
- Think clean lines and plenty of light

**Goal:** Every shot should feel like an invitation, highlighting how our spaces are built to help our customers create, connect, and grow

#### TEAM PORTRAITS
**Purpose:** Reflect the energy and dedication of our people

**Requirements:**
- Candid and natural
- Capture team members in action:
  - Organizing warehouse spaces
  - Assisting customers as they set up
  - Enjoying team building moments

**Goal:** Highlight the hands-on, approachable nature of our team and their commitment to creating a supportive environment for our customers

### 5.4 Cropping Guidelines

**Focus on the subject:**
- Always crop to emphasize the primary subject of the image
- Avoid leaving unnecessary space around the subject unless it adds to the composition
- Highlight the most striking parts of the image to create a strong visual connection

**Avoid cutting key elements:**
- Do not crop out important parts of the subject (hands, faces, focal points)
- Unless serves an intentional artistic purpose

**Use the rule of thirds:**
- Align key elements along the gridlines or at intersections of a rule-of-thirds grid
- Creates visually appealing compositions

**Zoom in for impact:**
- Tight cropping can emphasize key details and add drama to the composition

**Preserve context:**
- Ensure the crop retains enough background or surroundings to provide necessary context
- Especially if the setting is relevant to the message

**Avoid over-cropping:**
- Ensure the resolution remains high enough for the image's intended use
- Over-cropping can lead to pixelation or loss of detail

### 7.1 Sentence Case Mandate
**RULE:** All headlines set in sentence case (not title case, not uppercase)

**Correct:** "Holiday shipping deadlines for businesses"
**Incorrect:** "Holiday Shipping Deadlines For Businesses"
**Incorrect:** "HOLIDAY SHIPPING DEADLINES"

### 7.2 Emphasis Rules
**For italic emphasis in headlines:**
- Use Besley Regular Italic or Besley Medium Italic
- Emphasize singular word, verb, or adjective
